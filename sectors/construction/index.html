Engineers, Quantity Surveyors, and Construction Professionals

Civil engineers, structural engineers, site engineers, quantity surveyors, construction project managers, and BIM coordinators may qualify for the Critical Skills Employment Permit at a minimum of €40,904 per year. The CSEP has one significant advantage over the GEP: no Labour Market Needs Test is required. This can reduce the overall timeline by 4 to 6 weeks. Our Work Permit Guide covers both permit routes in detail.

What About General Labourers and Construction Operatives?

A common question from Irish construction employers is whether general labourers or general site operatives can be hired from outside the EEA on an employment permit. The answer is no. DETE's Ineligible Occupations List explicitly excludes elementary construction occupations (SOC 9120) and other construction operatives not elsewhere classified (SOC 8149). These roles cannot be sponsored for any type of employment permit. If an agency tells you otherwise, they are wrong — and you will lose your €1,000 DETE fee when the application is refused. CA Recruitment will not take your money to submit an application that cannot succeed. If you are unsure whether your specific role qualifies, ask us and we will check at no cost.

English is an official language of the Philippines and taught throughout the school system. Instructions from foremen, safety briefings, toolbox talks, and working alongside Irish and other EEA workers — communication is not a problem.

International Construction Experience

Many Filipino tradespeople have worked on large-scale projects in the Gulf, the Middle East, and across Asia. They understand deadlines, site discipline, and working to international standards — not just local small-scale work.

Long-Term Retention

Workers on a General Employment Permit are tied to the role for the first 9 months under Employment Permits Regulations 2024. Most stay well beyond that. You get consistent site presence — not a revolving door of short-termers who leave the moment something better comes along.

How the Process Works

Five steps from your first call to your worker on site:

  • 1

    Free Eligibility Check

    We confirm your role qualifies, check your 50/50 workforce ratio (at least 50% EEA nationals is required for both the GEP and the CSEP), and walk you through what the process will involve. No obligation, no charge. If the role does not qualify, we tell you clearly and save everyone time.

  • 2

    Labour Market Needs Test

    For GEP roles, DETE requires 28 consecutive days of advertising on the DSP Employment Services/EURES network (jobsireland.ie) and one additional online platform before the permit application is submitted. We manage this from start to finish — placing the ads, monitoring responses, and building the evidence file. CSEP roles (professional grades) skip this step entirely.

  • 3

    Candidate Sourcing & Your Shortlist

    While the LMNT runs, we source in the Philippines. We screen candidates against your site requirements, check trade experience and references, and give you a shortlist. You interview. You choose. We do not send you a pile of unvetted CVs and leave you to figure it out.

  • 4

    Permit Application & D Visa

    We prepare and submit the DETE permit application. Once approved, we coordinate the employment D visa from the Philippine end. DETE publishes live processing dates at enterprise.gov.ie — we will keep you updated throughout so you are not left guessing.

  • 5

    Arrival, Safe Pass & On-Site

    When the worker arrives in Ireland, we assist with IRP registration through Immigration Service Delivery (ISD), PPS number, and bank account setup. We advise on Safe Pass — a legal requirement for all construction site workers in Ireland under the Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (Construction) Regulations 2013, completed after arrival. We check in for the first 90 days. Replacement is free if anything does not work out in that period.
