Agricultural Labour in Ireland

Ireland's agricultural sector faces a persistent and well-documented labour shortage. From dairy farms across Munster and Connacht to mushroom and horticulture operations in the Midlands, Irish farmers and food producers struggle to find and retain reliable staff from the local and EEA labour market.

Filipino agricultural workers have become an increasingly important part of the solution. They come with strong work ethics, relevant practical experience, and an openness to rural living and shift-based schedules that many local workers find unappealing. CA Recruitment places vetted Filipino workers into agricultural roles across Ireland — managing the employment permit process from start to finish.

The Permit Process for Agricultural Roles

Most agricultural roles in Ireland are filled via the General Employment Permit route. Some specialist or higher-paid roles may qualify for the Critical Skills Employment Permit. We'll advise on the appropriate route during your consultation.

  • 1

    Free Consultation

    We assess the role, your current workforce, and which permit route applies. We explain the 50/50 workforce requirement and confirm your eligibility. No obligation, no charge.

  • 2

    Candidate Sourcing, Vetting & Labour Market Test

    We source candidates in the Philippines and manage the 28-day labour market advertising requirement (Jobs Ireland/EURES + one additional platform) for the General Employment Permit before the application is submitted.

  • 3

    Placement & Post-Arrival Support

    Once the permit is approved and the worker arrives, we provide onboarding support and check in during the first 90 days. Replacement is free if anything goes wrong in this period.

Why Filipino Agricultural Workers?

Strong Work Ethic

Filipino workers are widely known for reliability, commitment to the job, and willingness to work the kind of early-morning and weekend schedules that farming demands.

English Proficiency

English is an official language of the Philippines and taught throughout the education system. Communication on the farm is rarely an issue.

Relevant Experience

Many Filipino candidates come with direct experience of agricultural work — dairy, crops, horticulture, and livestock. We source candidates with the practical knowledge your operation needs.

Cultural Adaptability

Filipino workers adapt well to rural Irish life. Many are accustomed to tight-knit community environments and the pace of farm work.

Yes. Most agricultural roles in Ireland can be filled via the General Employment Permit (GEP). The role must appear on the DETE eligible occupations list, the minimum salary threshold must be met (currently €36,605 per annum), and you must pass a 28-day Labour Market Needs Test showing the role cannot be filled locally. We manage this entire process for you.

Dairy farm operatives, pig unit workers, horticulture staff, mushroom pickers, harvest workers, and general farm hands are among the agricultural roles regularly placed via the GEP route. If your specific role is not listed, contact us — we'll confirm eligibility based on the current DETE occupations list.

From initial consultation to the worker starting on your farm, the process typically takes 3–5 months. The Labour Market Needs Test alone requires 28 consecutive days of advertising. Check current processing dates at enterprise.gov.ie — DETE processes applications in date order of receipt and times change regularly.

You pay no fee to CA Recruitment until the worker successfully arrives and starts on your farm. The employer-paid DETE permit fee (currently €1,000 for a GEP) is the main upfront cost. We'll outline all costs clearly during your free consultation.
