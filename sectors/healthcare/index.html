Healthcare Staffing in Ireland

Ireland's healthcare system faces a chronic nursing and healthcare assistant shortage that shows no sign of easing. The Philippines is one of the world's leading exporters of trained nurses — with internationally recognised qualifications, high English proficiency, and extensive hospital experience. Filipino nurses and healthcare workers have long been integral to the Irish healthcare system.

CA Recruitment places Filipino healthcare workers with Irish hospitals, nursing homes, private clinics, and community care settings, managing the employment permit application, NMBI registration support, and the full process from sourcing to first shift.

Note on nursing registration: Nurses wishing to work in Ireland must register with the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland (NMBI) before they can practice. We support our nursing candidates through the NMBI registration process as part of our service.

The Permit Process for Healthcare Roles

Many nursing and allied health professional roles qualify for the Critical Skills Employment Permit, which does not require a labour market needs test and allows family members to work in Ireland. Healthcare assistant roles typically use the General Employment Permit route.

  • 3

Why Filipino Healthcare Workers?

World-Class Training

Filipino nursing education follows internationally recognised standards. Many nurses hold BSc degrees and have years of hospital experience before emigrating.

English Proficiency

English is a medium of instruction in Filipino nursing programmes. Clinical communication, documentation, and patient interaction are all in English from the start.

Compassionate Care

Filipino healthcare workers are widely recognised for their patient-centred approach and their natural empathy in care settings — qualities that can't be taught in a classroom.

Retention Rates

Filipino nurses placed through the permit route are typically committed to staying. The investment in bringing them over creates a different level of loyalty than agency or temporary staff.

Registered nurses who meet NMBI (Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland) registration requirements and earn above the Critical Skills salary threshold may apply via the Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP). Healthcare assistants and other clinical support roles typically qualify for the General Employment Permit (GEP) at the minimum salary of €36,605 per annum.
