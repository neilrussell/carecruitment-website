Retail Staffing Challenges in Ireland

Irish retail has faced persistent recruitment difficulties across all store formats — from independent shops to large supermarket chains. High turnover, part-time churn, and difficulty filling consistent full-time roles have left many operators short-staffed at floor level and in supervisory positions.

Filipino workers bring a strong work ethic, genuine customer service orientation, and excellent English — all qualities that translate directly into a retail environment. Many have prior retail and customer service experience in the Philippines or internationally, and they tend to stay in roles once placed through the employment permit route.

Why Filipino Retail Workers?

Customer Service Orientation

Filipino culture places great emphasis on courtesy and service. This carries directly into retail — customer interactions, complaint handling, and general floor presence are natural strengths.

English Proficiency

English is an official language in the Philippines and is used in schools, business, and media. Customer communication and team coordination are never a barrier.

Reliability

Workers who come through the employment permit route have invested significantly in the opportunity. Consistent attendance and commitment to the role are the norm, not the exception.

Stable Workforce

Unlike locally sourced seasonal or part-time retail workers, permit holders tend to stay. Lower turnover means less disruption and reduced ongoing recruitment cost for your store.
