What an overseas recruitment agency in Ireland actually does

The term covers a range of service levels. Some agencies identify candidates and hand off the permit work to the employer. CA Recruitment manages the entire process:

  • Eligibility check: We confirm whether your role qualifies for a General Employment Permit and whether your workforce composition meets DETE's 50/50 requirement.
  • Labour Market Needs Test (LMNT): We manage the mandatory 28-day job advertising requirement on the DSP Jobs Ireland network and a second commercial platform. We log all applications and outcomes. You do not place any adverts yourself.
  • Candidate sourcing and vetting: We source directly from the Philippines through Monette's networks, verify qualifications, check references, and present a shortlist. You choose the person you want to hire.
  • DETE permit application: We prepare and submit the full application through the DETE Employment Permits Online system. You review and sign. DETE liaises with us.
  • D-visa coordination: Once the permit is approved, we coordinate the Irish D-visa process from the Philippines, track the worker's documentation, and make sure nothing delays departure.
  • Arrival support: We support the worker through Irish Residence Permit (IRP) registration, PPS number, and bank account setup on arrival. We stay in contact for the first 90 days.

Sectors we recruit for

DETE maintains an Eligible Occupations List that sets out which roles qualify for a General Employment Permit (GEP). CA Recruitment works across four main sectors where Filipino workers have a proven track record with Irish employers.

Healthcare and care

Care assistants, home carers, and disability support workers. The GEP minimum for care roles is €16.12/hr (€32,691 per annum on a 39-hour week). Many overseas applicants hold nursing or healthcare qualifications, which makes onboarding faster for care home operators and home care providers.

Hospitality — chefs

Executive Chef, Head Chef, Sous Chef, Chef de Partie, and Commis Chef grades. Minimum experience requirements apply at each grade. Front-of-house, bar, and kitchen porter roles do not qualify for employment permits under current DETE rules.

Construction

Trades and skilled construction roles eligible under the GEP. CA Recruitment has placed overseas workers in construction operations and manages the full permit process for contractors across Ireland.

Agriculture

Dairy farm workers, pig unit operatives, mushroom farm workers, horticulture workers, and general farm labourers. DETE quotas for agricultural roles are open and CA Recruitment has existing placements with Irish farms across Leinster and Munster.

The permit process in brief

The route for Filipino workers coming to Ireland is the General Employment Permit (GEP), issued by DETE (Ireland's Department of Enterprise, Tourism and Employment). Here are the key requirements:

  • Eligible occupation: The role must appear on DETE's Eligible Occupations List.
  • Minimum salary: €36,605 per annum for most roles. Care assistants, home carers, horticulture workers, and certain other categories qualify at the reduced threshold of €16.12/hr (€32,691 per annum on a 39-hour week).
  • Labour Market Needs Test: 28 consecutive days of advertising on Jobs Ireland (DSP) and one additional commercial platform, with a full application log.
  • 50/50 rule: At least 50% of your total workforce must be EEA nationals at the time of the permit application. Limited exemptions apply for start-ups and sole-employee situations.
  • DETE application fee: €1,000, paid by the employer at submission. This cannot be recovered from the worker.
  • Processing time: Currently approximately 10 to 12 weeks for new GEP applications. Check live processing dates at enterprise.gov.ie.

After DETE approves the GEP, the worker applies for an Irish D-visa at the Irish Embassy in Manila. On arrival in Ireland, they register with Immigration Service Delivery (ISD) to receive their Irish Residence Permit (IRP). The full process, from initial consultation to worker starting, typically takes 6 to 8 months.
