Employment Permit Processing Times in Ireland
An Employer's Tracker

How long does an Irish employment permit take? DETE's processing dates change weekly — here's where to read the current figures and what the full hiring timeline really looks like.

In short: DETE publishes current employment permit processing dates and updates them regularly. We don't quote a fixed number here because it changes — check the live dates, and read on for the realistic end-to-end timeline. CA Recruitment tracks the current dates against your application and keeps your hire on schedule.

Where to see current DETE processing times

The Department of Enterprise, Tourism and Employment (DETE) publishes its current employment permit processing dates and updates them regularly — usually weekly. The dates tell you which applications DETE is processing right now, broken down by permit type and by applicant type (Trusted Partner applications are processed ahead of standard ones).

Always check the live figures here: DETE current application processing dates. We don't quote a fixed number of weeks on this page because it changes — quoting a stale figure would only mislead you.

Processing time is only part of the timeline

The DETE decision is one stage of the hire, not the whole thing. A realistic end-to-end timeline — from your first call to the worker starting on site — is about 6 to 8 months, covering:

  • the 28-day Labour Market Needs Test (where required);
  • DETE processing of the permit (the published dates above);
  • the worker's D-visa; and
  • travel, IRP registration and onboarding.

Trusted Partner applications are faster

Employers registered as a DETE Trusted Partner have their applications processed ahead of standard applications and don't resubmit company documents each time. CA Recruitment manages applications to make use of the faster lane where you qualify.
