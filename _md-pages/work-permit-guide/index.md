# How to Hire Overseas Workers in Ireland — Work Permit Guide

CA Recruitment manages the full DETE employment permit process for Irish and UK employers. This guide explains every step, both permit types, the salary thresholds, and what employers are responsible for.

## What Is an Irish Employment Permit?

An Irish employment permit is a legal authorisation that allows a non-EEA national to work in Ireland in a specific job with a specific employer. Permits are issued by the Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment (DETE). Without a valid permit, it is illegal to employ a non-EEA national in Ireland.

## The Two Main Permit Types

### General Employment Permit (GEP)

- For most non-EEA occupations that are experiencing a labour shortage
- Requires a Labour Market Needs Test (28-day advertising on Jobs Ireland + one additional platform)
- Minimum annual salary: €36,605 (standard rate)
- Reduced rate for shortage occupations: €16.12/hr — €32,691/yr on a 39-hour week, €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week (healthcare support workers, home carers, horticulture workers)
- DETE processing time: 8 to 12 weeks from submission
- End-to-end timeline including LMNT: 6 to 8 months (care/hospitality); 6 to 8 months (agri/construction)
- Government fee paid by employer: €1,000 for permits up to 24 months
- Worker must remain with the sponsoring employer for 9 months before changing jobs
- 50/50 rule applies: at least 50% of the employer's total workforce must be EEA nationals at time of application

### Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP)

- For high-demand, skilled occupations on the DETE Critical Skills Occupations List
- No Labour Market Needs Test required — saves approximately 4 to 6 weeks
- Minimum annual salary: €40,904 for listed occupations; €68,911 for occupations not on the list
- End-to-end timeline: 2 to 4 months
- After the CSEP expires, the worker can apply for Stamp 4, giving broader work rights without renewing a permit
- Spouse and dependent children can apply to join the worker in Ireland under the Dependant/Partner/Spouse Employment Permit scheme
- 50/50 rule applies

## The Labour Market Needs Test (LMNT)

Required before submitting a General Employment Permit application. Steps:

1. Advertise the vacancy on Jobs Ireland (jobsireland.ie — DSP Employment Services/EURES network) for a minimum of 28 consecutive days
2. Advertise on at least one additional online jobs platform during the same period
3. Document all applications received
4. Record reasons for not progressing any Irish or EEA candidates
5. Retain all documentation — DETE may request it during processing

If the employer's 50/50 ratio drops during the LMNT period, the full 28-day test must be rerun once the ratio recovers.

## The 50/50 Rule

At the time of any employment permit application, at least 50% of the employer's total workforce must be EEA nationals. This applies to:

- Both GEP and CSEP
- All business sizes — no exemption for small employers
- All locations and departments combined

Two exceptions:
1. The non-EEA worker will be the sole employee of the business
2. The employer is an Enterprise Ireland or IDA Ireland-backed start-up registered within the previous 2 years with a formal EI/IDA letter of support

The ratio is calculated by headcount (full-time and part-time employees count equally) on the date of application submission.

## Minimum Salary Thresholds (2026)

| Permit type and role | Minimum annual salary |
|---|---|
| CSEP — listed occupation | €40,904 |
| CSEP — any occupation (not on list) | €68,911 |
| GEP — standard rate | €36,605 |
| GEP — healthcare support workers / home carers | €32,691 (39-hr week — hourly rate of €16.12 governs; 40-hr week requires €33,529.60) |
| GEP — horticulture workers | €32,691 (39-hr week basis) |

All permit holders must also receive at least the National Minimum Wage (€14.15/hour as of January 2026) for all hours worked. Whichever is higher — the NMW or the permit threshold — is the binding minimum. The qualifying salary must be basic pay: guaranteed premium payments can count towards it, but bonuses, shift allowances, and overtime cannot.

## Employer Obligations After Placement

- Pay at least the permit minimum salary (or NMW, whichever is higher)
- Provide the worker with the same employment rights as any other employee
- Notify DETE within 4 weeks if employment ends before the permit expires and submit a copy of the permit
- Maintain the 50/50 EEA workforce ratio — if it drops below 50%, no new permit applications can be submitted until it recovers
- The permit is tied to the specific employer and role — a new application is required if the job or employer changes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between a General Employment Permit and a Critical Skills Employment Permit?**
The CSEP is for high-demand, skilled occupations on the CSEP Occupations List. It requires no Labour Market Needs Test, processes faster, and allows family members to join the worker in Ireland. The GEP covers a much wider range of occupations but requires the 28-day Labour Market Needs Test and has lower salary thresholds.

**What are the minimum salary thresholds for Irish employment permits?**
As of April 2026: CSEP — €40,904 (listed occupations) or €68,911 (unlisted); GEP standard — €36,605; GEP shortage occupations (care assistants, home carers, horticulture) — €16.12/hr, which is €32,691 on a 39-hour week or €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week.

**How long does it take to hire an overseas worker in Ireland?**
GEP route: typically 6 to 8 months for care and hospitality; 6 to 8 months for agriculture and construction. CSEP route: typically 2 to 4 months. The GEP includes a mandatory 28-day LMNT before the application can be submitted.

**What is the Labour Market Needs Test for Irish employment permits?**
The LMNT requires advertising on Jobs Ireland for a minimum of 28 consecutive days plus at least one additional online platform. The employer must document all applications and reasons for not progressing Irish or EEA candidates. Required only for General Employment Permits.

**Who pays the employment permit fee?**
The employer pays the government permit fee — €1,000 for a GEP up to 24 months. It is a criminal offence under the Employment Permits Acts to pass this cost on to the worker.

**Can a worker change employer after getting an Irish employment permit?**
GEP holders must stay with the sponsoring employer for 9 months. After 9 months, a change requires a new permit application. CSEP holders can apply for a new CSEP with a different employer after 12 months, or apply for Stamp 4 after the CSEP expires.

**What does an employer need to do in the permit process?**
Sign the permit application as sponsoring employer; have a valid employment contract ready; meet the minimum salary; pass the 50/50 ratio check; run the LMNT (GEP only). CA Recruitment handles the application, documentation, and DETE liaison.

**What if the permit is refused?**
Refusals typically result from: role not meeting the salary threshold, LMNT not properly conducted, or the employer not satisfying the 50/50 rule. DETE issues a formal refusal with reasons. CA Recruitment can advise on whether an appeal or a fresh application is the right response.
