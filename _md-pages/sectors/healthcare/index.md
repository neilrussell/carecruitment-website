# Filipino Healthcare Workers for Ireland — CA Recruitment

CA Recruitment places vetted Filipino healthcare assistants and home support workers with Irish healthcare providers. Registered nurses are placed via the Critical Skills Employment Permit route with NMBI registration support. No fee until placement.

## Roles Filled

- Healthcare Assistants
- Home Support Workers
- Registered Nurses (CSEP route — NMBI registration required)

## Permit and Salary Information

**Healthcare Assistants and Home Support Workers (GEP)**
- **Permit type:** General Employment Permit
- **Minimum pay:** €16.12/hr — €32,691/yr on a 39-hour week, €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week (DETE shortage occupation rate; basic pay only — bonuses, shift allowances, and overtime do not count)
- **Labour Market Needs Test:** required — 28-day advertising on Jobs Ireland + one additional platform
- **50/50 rule:** yes
- **Timeline:** 6 to 8 months

**Registered Nurses (CSEP)**
- **Permit type:** Critical Skills Employment Permit (SOC 2231)
- **Minimum annual salary:** €40,904
- **Labour Market Needs Test:** not required
- **NMBI registration:** required before practising in Ireland — CA Recruitment advises on the NMBI registration pathway
- **Timeline:** 2 to 4 months (permit); NMBI registration is a parallel process

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I hire overseas healthcare workers in Ireland?**
Yes. Healthcare assistants and home support workers qualify for the General Employment Permit at a minimum of €16.12 per hour — €32,691 per year on a 39-hour week, or €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week. Registered nurses on the Critical Skills Occupations List may qualify for the CSEP.

**What healthcare roles qualify for an employment permit in Ireland?**
Healthcare assistants and home support workers qualify for the GEP. Registered nurses (SOC 2231) appear on the Critical Skills Occupations List and may qualify for the CSEP. Eligibility for other roles is confirmed on a case-by-case basis during the free consultation.

**Do Filipino nurses need to re-register to work in Ireland?**
Yes. Nurses from the Philippines must register with the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Ireland (NMBI) before they can practise in Ireland. NMBI assesses qualifications from the Philippines. CA Recruitment advises on the NMBI registration pathway and supports candidates through this process.

**Is there a Labour Market Needs Test for healthcare recruitment?**
It depends on the permit type. The GEP (used for healthcare assistants and home support workers) requires a 28-day LMNT. The CSEP (used for registered nurses) does not require an LMNT.

**Does the 50/50 workforce rule apply to nursing homes and care homes?**
Yes. At least 50% of your workforce must be EEA nationals at the time of the permit application. This applies to nursing homes, care homes, home care providers, and disability services.

**How long does it take to hire an overseas healthcare worker?**
For GEP roles (healthcare assistants, home support workers): 6 to 8 months. For CSEP roles (registered nurses): 2 to 4 months for the permit. NMBI registration runs in parallel and timelines vary.

**What salary do I need to pay?**
Healthcare assistants and home support workers: €16.12/hr minimum — €32,691/yr on a 39-hour week, €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week (GEP shortage occupation rate). The qualifying salary must be basic pay: guaranteed premium payments can count, but bonuses, shift allowances, and overtime cannot. Registered nurses: €40,904 minimum (CSEP). All permit holders must also receive at least the National Minimum Wage for all hours worked.

**How much does it cost to hire through CA Recruitment?**
No recruitment fee until the worker arrives and starts. The main external cost is the DETE permit fee — €1,000 for a GEP.
