# Filipino Agricultural Workers for Ireland — CA Recruitment

CA Recruitment places vetted Filipino agricultural workers with Irish farms and agri-businesses. Permit type: General Employment Permit. Timeline: 6 to 8 months.

## Roles Filled

- Dairy Farm Operatives
- Pig Unit Workers
- Horticulture Workers
- Mushroom Pickers and Harvest Workers
- General Farm Hands
- Agricultural Labourers

## Permit and Salary Information

- **Permit type:** General Employment Permit (GEP)
- **Minimum annual salary (most agri roles):** €36,605
- **Minimum annual salary (horticulture):** €32,691 (DETE reduced rate for this category)
- **National Minimum Wage floor:** €14.15/hour from January 2026 — whichever is higher applies
- **Labour Market Needs Test:** required — 28-day advertising on Jobs Ireland + one additional platform
- **50/50 rule:** yes — at least 50% of the farm's workforce must be EEA nationals at time of application
- **Government permit fee:** €1,000 paid by employer to DETE

## Timeline

From initial consultation to the worker starting on the farm: **6 to 8 months**. This includes the 28-day LMNT, 8 to 12 weeks of DETE processing, visa approval, and travel from the Philippines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I legally hire overseas agricultural workers in Ireland?**
Yes. Most agricultural roles qualify for the General Employment Permit. The role must appear on DETE's eligible occupations list, meet the minimum salary threshold, and pass a 28-day Labour Market Needs Test showing the position cannot be filled locally.

**What agricultural roles qualify for an employment permit?**
Dairy farm operatives, pig unit workers, horticulture workers, mushroom pickers, harvest workers, and general farm hands. Contact CA Recruitment to confirm eligibility for specific roles against the current DETE occupations list.

**How long does it take to hire an overseas farm worker in Ireland?**
From initial consultation to the worker starting on the farm: 6 to 8 months. This includes the 28-day Labour Market Needs Test, DETE permit processing, visa approval, and travel from the Philippines.

**What salary do I need to pay a farm worker on a General Employment Permit?**
For most agricultural roles the GEP minimum annual salary is €36,605. Horticulture workers have a lower threshold of €32,691 under DETE's reduced rate for that category. You must also pay at least the Irish National Minimum Wage for all hours worked.

**Does the 50/50 workforce rule apply to farms?**
Yes. At least 50% of your workforce must be EEA nationals at the time of the permit application. This applies to farms of all sizes.

**Do Filipino farm workers speak English?**
Yes. English is an official language of the Philippines and is the primary medium of instruction throughout secondary and tertiary education.

**How much does it cost to hire through CA Recruitment?**
CA Recruitment's recruitment fee is agreed upfront and separate from government costs. The main external cost is the €1,000 employer-paid DETE General Employment Permit fee.

**What happens if the worker doesn't stay?**
Workers placed via an employment permit have relocated specifically for the role, so retention rates are typically strong.
