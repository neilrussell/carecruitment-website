# Filipino Construction Workers for Ireland — CA Recruitment

CA Recruitment places vetted Filipino construction workers with Irish contractors and construction businesses. Permit type: General Employment Permit (most roles). Timeline: 6 to 8 months. No fee until placement.

## Roles Filled

- General Construction Operatives
- Steel Fixers / Rebar Workers
- Groundworkers
- Plant Operators
- Scaffolders
- Construction Labourers

## Permit and Salary Information

- **Permit type:** General Employment Permit (GEP) for most site roles; Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP) for professional/engineering roles
- **Minimum pay:** the legally binding Construction Sector SEO rate where applicable — craftspersons €23.00/hour from 1 August 2025 (about €46,600/year at 39 hours), Category A €22.32/hour, Category B €20.71/hour. Electricians and plumbers fall under their own sector SEOs. The €36,605 GEP threshold is the permit floor; the SEO rate above it is what must actually be paid. Current rates: workplacerelations.ie
- **Labour Market Needs Test:** required for GEP — 28-day advertising on Jobs Ireland + one additional platform
- **50/50 rule:** yes — at least 50% of the company's workforce must be EEA nationals at time of application
- **Safe Pass:** required for all workers on Irish construction sites under the Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (Construction) Regulations 2013. Workers can complete Safe Pass training on arrival in Ireland before beginning site work.
- **Government permit fee:** €1,000 paid by employer to DETE

## Timeline

From initial consultation to the worker starting on site: **6 to 8 months**. This includes the 28-day LMNT, 8 to 12 weeks of DETE processing, visa approval, and travel from the Philippines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I hire Filipino construction workers in Ireland?**
Yes, for many construction roles. The General Employment Permit covers a range of construction trades and site roles. Contact CA Recruitment to confirm whether a specific role and title is on DETE's eligible occupations list.

**How long does it take to hire an overseas construction worker?**
For a General Employment Permit, the typical timeline from initial consultation to the worker starting on site is 6 to 8 months. This includes the 28-day Labour Market Needs Test, DETE permit processing, visa approval, and travel from the Philippines.

**Will overseas construction workers be ready to work on site immediately?**
Most are ready to start productive work from day one. Filipino construction workers placed through CA Recruitment are sourced for the specific role and duties on the site. Workers placed under GEP have the relevant occupational experience.

**Do overseas construction workers need Safe Pass in Ireland?**
Yes. Safe Pass is a legal requirement for all workers on Irish construction sites. It is administered by SOLAS. Workers can complete Safe Pass training on arrival in Ireland, typically within a few days of starting.

**Does the 50/50 workforce rule apply to construction companies?**
Yes. At least 50% of your workforce must be EEA nationals at the time of the permit application. This applies to both GEP and CSEP.

**How much does it cost to hire through CA Recruitment?**
No recruitment fee until the worker arrives and starts on site. The main external cost is the employer-paid DETE permit fee — currently €1,000 for a General Employment Permit.

**What if the construction worker doesn't work out?**
CA Recruitment provides a replacement at no additional recruitment fee if the worker leaves within the first 90 days.

**Is the overseas recruitment process complicated?**
From the employer's side: free consultation, reviewing a candidate shortlist, and conducting interviews. CA Recruitment manages the Labour Market Needs Test, permit documentation, DETE application, visa coordination, and arrival support.
