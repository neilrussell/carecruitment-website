## What We Do

CA Recruitment sources vetted Filipino workers and places them with Irish and UK employers across nine sectors: agriculture, construction, hospitality, healthcare, care and social services, logistics and warehousing, manufacturing, retail, and accounting and finance.

The agency manages the full DETE employment permit process end-to-end:

- Role assessment and permit eligibility check
- Candidate sourcing and vetting in the Philippines
- Labour Market Needs Test advertising and documentation (for General Employment Permits)
- Employment permit application preparation and submission through DETE's EPOS system
- DETE liaison and progress monitoring
- Visa coordination once the permit is approved
- Pre-departure preparation for the worker
- Arrival support and first-90-days onboarding

**No placement fee is charged until the worker starts.** A 90-day replacement guarantee applies to every placement.

## Why Filipino Workers

Filipino workers have a well-established reputation in Ireland and the UK across healthcare, hospitality, agriculture, construction, and care. Key factors:

- **English proficiency:** English is an official language of the Philippines and the primary medium of instruction throughout secondary and tertiary education. Most candidates are fully comfortable working in English from day one.
- **Work ethic and reliability:** Filipino workers placed through CA Recruitment have undergone qualification verification and reference checks before the shortlist is presented to any employer.
- **Established permit infrastructure:** The Philippines has a long-standing overseas employment system (POEA/DMW). Workers are familiar with the process, documentation requirements, and what overseas placement involves. This significantly reduces no-shows and last-minute withdrawals.
- **Cultural fit:** Filipino workers adapt well to Irish workplace culture. Many already have family or community connections in Ireland from previous placements in the healthcare and hospitality sectors.
- **Retention:** Workers placed via employment permit are committed to the role — they have relocated specifically for it. GEP and CSEP holders must remain with their sponsoring employer for 9 months under Irish law, giving employers a stable initial period.

## Our Model

CA Recruitment operates as a managed placement service, not a CV database. Every candidate goes through sourcing, qualification verification, reference checking, and video interview coordination before reaching an employer's shortlist. Employers receive pre-screened people, not raw applications.

The fee model is straightforward: nothing until placement. The only upfront cost to the employer is the DETE government permit fee (€1,000 for most General Employment Permits), which is paid directly to DETE and has nothing to do with CA Recruitment's fee.
