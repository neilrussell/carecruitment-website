How permit salary thresholds work in 2026

To hire a worker from outside the EU/EEA, the salary you offer has to meet a legal minimum before the employment permit can be approved. Which minimum applies depends on the role — there isn't a single number.

Standard General Employment Permit roles — €36,605/year

Most permit-eligible roles — chefs, HGV drivers, welders, machinists and many others — sit on the standard General Employment Permit floor of €36,605 per year (raised from €34,000 on 1 March 2026). That floor is based on a 39-hour week. If the week is shorter the hourly rate must rise so the annual minimum is still met, and each hour above 39 must be paid at no less than the prescribed hourly rate (€18.05 for a standard GEP).

How hours affect it: permit salary thresholds are built around a standard full-time week of 39 hours (2,028 hours a year). DETE will issue a permit for a role under 39 hours — but not below 20 hours a week — as long as the hourly rate is raised so the worker still earns the full annual minimum. In practice almost every permit role is full-time at 39 hours.

Shortage roles — €16.12/hour

A reduced hourly minimum of €16.12 per hour applies to designated shortage occupations: healthcare assistants, home carers and home support workers, horticulture workers and meat processing operatives. That works out at €32,691 per year on a 39-hour week or €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week. It must be basic pay — bonuses, shift allowances and overtime do not count towards the threshold.

Construction trades — Sectoral Employment Order rates

Construction is different again. Trades are governed by Sectoral Employment Orders (SEOs), which set legally binding minimum hourly rates that are higher than the general permit floor. The Construction Sector SEO craftsperson rate is €23.00 per hour from 1 August 2025 — about €46,600 per year on a 39-hour week — covering trades such as carpenters, bricklayers, plasterers and glaziers. Other construction workers such as scaffolders and steel fixers are Category A workers at €22.32 per hour. Electricians fall under the Electrical Contracting SEO and plumbers under the Mechanical Engineering Building Services SEO, at similar or higher rates.

Critical Skills roles — €40,904/year

Higher-skilled occupations on the Critical Skills Occupations List have a minimum of €40,904 per year and follow a faster route that does not require a Labour Market Needs Test.

Every role, whichever band it falls in, must also be paid at least the National Minimum Wage (€14.15 per hour in 2026) for all hours worked.
