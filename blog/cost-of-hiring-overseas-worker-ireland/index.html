How Much Does It Cost to Hire an Overseas Worker in Ireland in 2026?

25 May 2026  ·  7 min read  ·  By CA Recruitment

The short answer: budget between €3,500 and €9,000 all-in for a straightforward General Employment Permit hire, depending on the role, the salary, and whether you use a recruitment agency. Here is exactly what makes up that figure.

The DETE permit application fee: €1,000

The Government's fee for a General Employment Permit (GEP) is €1,000, paid when you submit the application to DETE. The permit, if granted, is valid for up to 24 months. There is no additional charge to renew.

If the application is refused, DETE retains a portion of the fee and refunds the remainder. The exact refund depends on what stage the application was refused — if it is rejected at validation stage, a larger proportion is refunded. If it goes to a full decision and is refused on merits, less comes back. DETE publishes the current fee schedule on enterprise.gov.ie.

One point employers often miss: the permit cost cannot be passed on to the worker. Under the Employment Permits Acts, deducting permit costs from a worker's wages is a criminal offence. This is the employer's cost.

The salary commitment: at least €36,605 per year

This is not a one-off cost, but it shapes everything else. From 1 March 2026, the minimum annual salary for a GEP is €36,605 for most roles. A lower threshold of €32,691 applies to care assistant roles, horticulture, and meat processing.

You need to be paying the worker at or above this threshold for the permit to issue. If the salary drops below the threshold at any point, there is a compliance problem. Factor this into your hiring decision before starting the process.

Sector note for care employers: Healthcare assistant and care worker roles qualify for the lower €32,691 threshold. This is a meaningful difference if you are running a nursing home or home care service with tight margins. See our guide to hiring overseas care assistants for more on the care-specific permit route.

The Labour Market Needs Test: €0 to €500

Before you can apply for a GEP, you must complete the Labour Market Needs Test (LMNT). This requires advertising the vacancy for 28 consecutive days on two platforms: the Department of Social Protection Employment Services/EURES network (Jobs Ireland satisfies this requirement, and is free) plus at least one additional commercial job platform. You keep a record of every application received and why EEA applicants were not selected.

The cost of commercial advertising varies. A job post on a mainstream Irish job board typically runs €200 to €500 depending on the site and the listing type. The LMNT adds 28 days to your timeline on top of the DETE processing period, so plan for it early.

If the role qualifies for a Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP), there is no LMNT — that step is skipped entirely. See our GEP vs CSEP guide to check whether your role qualifies.

Recruitment agency fee: €5,000 to €12,000

Most Irish employers who hire overseas workers use a recruitment agency to manage the process. The fee varies depending on the agency's model:

  • Percentage of first-year salary: typically 15% to 20% of the worker's annual salary. At €36,605 minimum salary that is €5,500 to €7,300.
  • Fixed fee: some agencies charge a flat fee per placement, which can be better value for higher-salary roles.
  • All-inclusive model: agencies like CA Recruitment bundle permit management into the placement fee. You pay once, and the agency handles the LMNT, DETE application, document collection, and compliance checks.

With CA Recruitment, you pay nothing until the worker starts. There is no upfront fee for the consultation, the sourcing, or the permit management. If the worker leaves within 90 days of starting, we replace at no charge.

Relocation costs: optional but common

Flights from Manila to Ireland typically run €800 to €1,500 return. Some employers cover the worker's initial flight as part of the offer; others do not. There is no legal requirement to pay for relocation, but in a competitive hiring environment it helps with candidate commitment.

Accommodation support is the bigger variable. Some employers arrange short-term accommodation for the first few weeks; others provide a housing allowance. Neither is legally required, but it reduces the risk of a worker dropping out after the permit is issued because they cannot find housing quickly.

Other costs employers miss

Medical screening

Filipino workers must obtain a medical certificate as part of their pre-departure documentation from the Philippines. This is a worker cost, not an employer cost.

Garda vetting for care roles

If you are hiring for a care or healthcare role, the worker will need Garda vetting before they can start. There is no charge to the employer for Garda vetting. Turnaround through the National Vetting Bureau is typically a few weeks, which can run in parallel with other post-permit steps.

NBI clearance from the Philippines

The worker's NBI (National Bureau of Investigation) clearance from the Philippines is the equivalent of an Irish Garda clearance. DETE requires this as part of the permit application. It is a worker cost and is standard for all Philippine permit applications.

Legal fees: not usually necessary

Most GEP applications do not require a solicitor. If your business has a complex ownership structure, has had previous WRC compliance issues, or if the application involves an unusual role, legal input may be needed. Budget €500 to €2,000 if this applies. For straightforward cases, a competent agency handles everything without legal involvement.

The return on that cost

Hiring managers in care, agriculture, and construction tell us the same thing: the cost of an overseas hire is significantly less than the cost of leaving a role unfilled for six months. Unfilled roles carry overtime costs, agency cover costs, reduced capacity, and in care settings, potential regulatory issues. One overseas placement typically pays for itself within two to three months.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a General Employment Permit cost in Ireland?

The DETE application fee is €1,000, paid at the time of application. The permit is valid for up to 24 months. The fee cannot be recovered from the worker.

What is the minimum salary for a sponsored overseas worker in Ireland?

€36,605 per year for most roles under a GEP from 1 March 2026. €32,691 for care assistant, horticulture, and meat processing roles. The role must appear on DETE's Eligible Occupations List.

Does the employer pay for the work permit in Ireland?

Yes. The DETE fee is an employer cost. You cannot pass it to the worker or deduct it from wages — that is a criminal offence under the Employment Permits Acts.

What does the Labour Market Needs Test cost?

Jobs Ireland (DSP Employment Services) advertising is free and satisfies the mandatory EURES/DSP network requirement. The additional commercial platform typically costs €200 to €500 depending on the site. The test takes 28 days and must be completed before the GEP application.

Is Garda vetting required for overseas care workers?

Yes. There is no employer charge for Garda vetting. It runs through the National Vetting Bureau and typically takes a few weeks.
