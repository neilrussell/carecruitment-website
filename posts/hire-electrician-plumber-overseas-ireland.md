<h2 id="short-answer">The short answer</h2>

<p>Yes. Irish employers can hire electricians, plumbers, and other qualified tradespeople from outside the EU. The route is a <a href="/work-permit-guide/">General Employment Permit (GEP)</a> issued by DETE (Ireland's Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment).</p>

<p>It is not a shortcut around local hiring. Before DETE will consider a permit application, you must advertise the role for 28 days and show that no suitable EEA candidate applied.</p>

<p>If the role qualifies and you follow the steps correctly, the process is more straightforward than most employers expect. The ones who hit trouble are usually the ones who skip the preparation — checking their 50/50 ratio, getting the LMNT documentation right — before they start.</p>

<h2 id="which-trades-qualify">Which trades qualify for a construction GEP?</h2>

<p>The GEP covers skilled and professional roles. DETE publishes a list of eligible occupations and a separate ineligible list. For construction, the key distinction is between skilled trades and elementary site labour.</p>

<p>GEP-eligible construction trades include:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Electricians</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Plumbers</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Pipefitters</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Welders</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Steel fixers</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Mechanical and electrical engineers</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Civil engineers</strong></li>
  <li><strong>MEP and structural engineering roles</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>What does not qualify: basic construction operatives classified under SOC code 9120 (elementary construction occupations). If a role requires no formal qualification and involves general site labour, it cannot be sponsored under a GEP.</p>

<p>DETE updated the GEP eligible occupations list in May 2026, adding further construction roles including steel fixers, concrete pump operators, curtain wallers, and fencing operators. If you are uncertain whether your specific role qualifies, check the <a href="https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/what-we-do/workplace-and-skills/employment-permits/employment-permit-eligibility/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">current eligible occupations list at enterprise.gov.ie</a> or <a href="https://wa.me/353894166124?text=Hi%2C%20I%27m%20looking%20to%20hire%20a%20tradesperson%20from%20overseas.%20Can%20you%20help%20with%20the%20permit%20process%3F" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WhatsApp Monette</a> before starting anything.</p>

<h2 id="lmnt">The Labour Market Needs Test</h2>

<p>Before DETE will accept a GEP application, you have to demonstrate that you gave EEA candidates a genuine shot at the role and nobody suitable applied. That is the LMNT. It runs before you submit anything to DETE.</p>

<p>What it involves:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Advertise the vacancy on <strong>Jobs Ireland</strong> (jobsireland.ie) — this is the mandatory DSP/EURES platform. There is no substitute for it.</li>
  <li>Advertise on <strong>at least one additional online platform</strong> — a commercial job board, LinkedIn, or any other site whose purpose is publishing job offers.</li>
  <li>Run both ads for the <strong>full 28 consecutive days</strong>. There is no shortcut.</li>
  <li>Keep a log of every application received during those 28 days.</li>
  <li>Document why no EEA applicant was suitable for the role.</li>
</ul>

<p>DETE checks this record at application stage. Gaps in the log — missing applications, no written reason why a candidate was rejected — are one of the most common reasons permits get refused or held up.</p>

<p>One practical point: the 28-day LMNT window is time you can use. CA Recruitment runs candidate sourcing in parallel — while the LMNT is running, we are screening Filipino candidates. By the time the 28 days close, you already have a shortlist ready.</p>

<div class="callout callout-gold">
  <p><strong>The 50/50 rule applies before you advertise.</strong> Before starting the LMNT, check that at least 50% of your total workforce are EEA nationals. If you run the full 28-day LMNT and then find out your ratio is below 50%, you cannot submit the application. That is four weeks of wasted effort. Check the ratio first. <h2 id="salary">Salary requirements: the SEO rate, not just the permit threshold</h2>

<p>This is the point that catches employers out most often. The GEP has a minimum annual salary of <strong>€36,605</strong>, but for trades roles that is not the number that matters. Construction trades in Ireland are covered by <strong>Sectoral Employment Orders (SEOs)</strong>, which set legally binding minimum hourly rates for the sector. The salary on the job offer and the permit application must meet the applicable SEO rate, and for qualified tradespeople the SEO rate works out well above the permit threshold.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Electricians</strong> are covered by the Electrical Contracting Sector SEO. The newly qualified electrician rate works out at roughly €47,000 per year on a 39-hour week, with higher rates from the third and sixth year of employment.</li>
  <li><strong>Plumbers and pipefitters</strong> are covered by the Mechanical Engineering Building Services Contracting Sector SEO, with craft rates at a similar level.</li>
  <li><strong>Other construction trades</strong> (bricklayers, carpenters, plasterers, glaziers, painters) fall under the Construction Sector SEO: the craftsperson rate is €23.00 per hour from 1 August 2025, which is about €46,600 per year on a 39-hour week.</li>
</ul>

<p>The SEOs also oblige the employer to make pension and sick pay scheme contributions on top of the hourly rate. These are not optional.</p>

<p>Two further points on how the salary is assessed:</p>

<ul>
  <li>The salary must be the <strong>contracted basic rate</strong>. You cannot meet it by adding overtime payments, subsistence allowances, or site bonuses to a lower base. DETE looks at the base salary figure in the employment contract.</li>
  <li>A job offer below the applicable SEO rate is an offer of employment below the legal minimum for the sector. Build the permit application on the SEO rate, not the GEP threshold.</li>
</ul>

<p>SEO rates are reviewed regularly, with the construction rates typically changing each August. Check the <a href="https://www.workplacerelations.ie/en/what_you_should_know/hours-and-wages/sectoral%20employment%20orders/construction-sector/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">current SEO rates at workplacerelations.ie</a> before setting the salary in a job offer or permit application.</p>

<h2 id="processing-times">DETE processing times</h2>

<p>Once the LMNT is complete and the GEP application is submitted to DETE, current processing time for new applications is approximately <strong>10 to 12 weeks</strong>.</p>

<p>This figure changes. Before planning your timeline, check the <a href="https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/what-we-do/workplace-and-skills/employment-permits/current-application-processing-dates/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">live processing dates on enterprise.gov.ie</a>. That page is updated regularly and is the only source you should rely on for current waiting times.</p>

<p>After DETE approves the permit, the worker applies for an Irish D-visa at the Irish Embassy in Manila. Embassy processing typically takes 2 to 4 weeks. The full process from first contact to worker on site is 6 to 8 months.</p>

<p>The Construction Industry Federation has been vocal about labour shortages pushing up costs and delaying projects across the country. That pressure is not going away. The employers who start the permit process now are the ones who have staff in place for Q3 and Q4.</p>
