<h1>How to Streamline Overseas Recruitment for Ireland in 2026</h1>

<p id="short-answer">Irish companies streamline overseas recruitment by running the stages in parallel instead of in sequence. Permit eligibility is confirmed before anything is advertised. Employer documents are prepared while the 28-day Labour Market Needs Test runs. Candidates are sourced and vetted while DETE processes the permit. Done this way, the full journey from decision to first day takes roughly six months. Done stage by stage, with rework, it can stretch well past a year.</p>

<p>This guide sets out the five things that actually shorten the process for an Irish employer in 2026, with the current figures from DETE and the Workplace Relations Commission. It applies whether you are hiring a healthcare assistant, a site craftsperson, a chef, or a farm operative.</p>

<h2 id="where-time-goes">Where the time goes in 2026</h2>

<p>Three parts of the process have fixed durations you cannot change:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>The Labour Market Needs Test (LMNT):</strong> 28 days of advertising before a General Employment Permit application can be submitted. It cannot be shortened.</li>
  <li><strong>DETE processing:</strong> as of June 2026, DETE is working through new General Employment Permit applications received around 11 March 2026. That is roughly a three-month queue. Check the <a href="https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/what-we-do/workplace-and-skills/employment-permits/current-application-processing-dates/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">live processing dates page</a> before you plan around any figure.</li>
  <li><strong>The entry visa and travel:</strong> for a non-EEA worker, the employment D-visa, pre-departure clearances, and travel typically add one to two months after the permit issues.</li>
</ul>

<p>Add those together and the floor is around six months. Everything else in the timeline is avoidable delay: applications returned for errors, advertising re-run because the LMNT was done wrong, candidates who fall through at the visa stage, or sourcing that only starts after the permit is approved. Streamlining means attacking the avoidable part, because the fixed part will not move.</p>

<h2 id="step-1">Step 1: Confirm permit eligibility before you advertise anything</h2>

<p>The most expensive mistake in overseas recruitment is discovering a problem after the clock has started. Three checks come first.</p>

<h3>Is the role eligible, and at what salary?</h3>

<p>The role must not appear on the Ineligible Occupations List, and the salary must meet the correct threshold for that specific role, not the generic one. In 2026:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Most General Employment Permit roles require a minimum of <strong>€36,605 per year</strong>.</li>
  <li>Healthcare assistants, home support workers, horticulture workers, and meat processing operatives qualify at a lower quota threshold of <strong>€16.12 per hour</strong>. That is €32,691 on a 39-hour week, and €33,529.60 on a 40-hour week. The hourly rate governs, and only basic pay counts towards it.</li>
  <li>Construction trades are covered by Sectoral Employment Orders, which sit above the permit threshold. A craftsperson must be paid at least <strong>€23.00 per hour</strong> under the Construction Sector SEO from 1 August 2025, which is roughly €46,600 per year on a 39-hour week. Quoting €36,605 for a trades role is wrong and will cause problems later.</li>
</ul>

<p>Thresholds are reviewed annually, so verify the current figures on <a href="https://enterprise.gov.ie/en/what-we-do/workplace-and-skills/employment-permits/permit-types/general-employment-permit/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">enterprise.gov.ie</a> before budgeting. Our guide to the <a href="/blog/minimum-salary-overseas-worker-ireland/">minimum salary for an overseas worker in Ireland</a> covers the thresholds role by role.</p>

<h3>Do you pass the 50:50 rule?</h3>

<p>At the time of application, at least 50% of your employees must be EEA nationals. There are only two exemptions: a business where the permit holder would be the sole employee, and a start-up under two years old with a formal letter of support from Enterprise Ireland or IDA Ireland. Many employers close to the threshold only find out when DETE refuses the application. Check it on day one. The <a href="/blog/50-50-rule-irish-employment-permits/">50:50 rule guide</a> explains the calculation.</p>

<h3>Is it the right permit type?</h3>

<p>Roles on the Critical Skills Occupations List use the Critical Skills Employment Permit and skip the Labour Market Needs Test entirely, which removes 28 days from the timeline. Most care, agriculture, construction, and hospitality roles go through the General Employment Permit route instead.</p>

<h2 id="step-2">Step 2: Prepare employer documents before the clock starts</h2>

<p>While the LMNT advertisement runs its 28 days, you can have every employer-side document ready to submit the moment it closes: Tax Clearance Certificate, company registration details, the employment contract at the correct salary, and the full record of the advertising. Employers who start gathering documents after the LMNT closes routinely lose two to four weeks here for no reason. The DETE application is submitted through the EPOS portal, and a complete application on the day the LMNT closes is the single easiest win in the whole process.</p>

<h2 id="step-3">Step 3: Run the Labour Market Needs Test once, properly</h2>

<p>The LMNT exists to show that no suitable Irish or EEA candidate was available. The advertisement must run for 28 days on Jobs Ireland, which also carries it to the EU-wide EURES network, plus at least one additional online platform. You must keep records of every application received, every interview held, and the reason each candidate was unsuitable.</p>

<p>A returned application because the advertising ran on the wrong platforms, for the wrong duration, or without proper records does not just cost the resubmission time. It usually means re-running the full 28 days. This is the stage where most do-it-yourself permit applications go wrong, and it is entirely preventable.</p>

<h2 id="step-4">Step 4: Source and vet candidates in parallel</h2>

<p>The permit application names a specific candidate, so sourcing has to start early, not after approval. The streamlined sequence looks like this: while the LMNT advertisement runs, candidate sourcing and shortlisting happen overseas. By the time the 28 days close, you have interviewed and selected your candidate, their qualifications and references are verified, and the candidate-side documents are ready to go into the same application.</p>

<p>Source country matters here. CA Recruitment recruits primarily from the Philippines, where hiring involves compliance on both sides: the Irish permit process, and the Philippine side of DMW accreditation, Overseas Employment Certificate processing, and pre-departure medical clearance. An agency that understands only the Irish half will lose weeks on the half it does not understand. The same principle applies to any source country: someone on your side needs to know the exit process as well as the entry process.</p>

<p>For role-specific detail, see our guides on <a href="/blog/how-to-hire-overseas-workers-ireland/">how to hire overseas workers in Ireland</a> and the full <a href="/work-permit-guide/">work permit guide for Irish employers</a>.</p>

<h2 id="step-5">Step 5: Plan the visa and arrival stage before the permit issues</h2>

<p>The permit being granted is not the finish line. A visa-required national then applies for an employment D-visa, which typically takes four to eight weeks, followed by travel, Irish Residence Permit (IRP) registration with Immigration Service Delivery, and a PPS number application after arrival. Employers who treat this stage as an afterthought add a month at the worst possible time, when the start date is already promised to rosters and contracts.</p>

<p>Streamlined version: the visa application file is prepared while the permit is still in the DETE queue, pre-departure steps are booked the week the permit issues, and the first weeks of onboarding (IRP appointment, PPS application, accommodation, induction) are planned before the flight is booked.</p>

<h2 id="timeline">A realistic 2026 timeline</h2>

<p>Run in parallel, the process looks like this for a General Employment Permit hire:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Weeks 1 to 2:</strong> eligibility checks, 50:50 ratio confirmed, salary threshold confirmed, LMNT advertising placed. Document preparation begins.</li>
  <li><strong>Weeks 2 to 6:</strong> LMNT runs its 28 days. Candidate sourcing, interviews, and vetting happen in parallel. Employer documents completed.</li>
  <li><strong>Week 6:</strong> complete application submitted to DETE the week the LMNT closes.</li>
  <li><strong>Weeks 6 to 19:</strong> DETE processing, currently around three months for new applications. Visa file prepared during this window.</li>
  <li><strong>Weeks 19 to 27:</strong> D-visa processing, pre-departure clearances, travel, and arrival.</li>
</ul>

<p>That is roughly six months end to end, and it assumes nothing is returned for rework. Anyone quoting a materially shorter timeline for a visa-required General Employment Permit hire in 2026 is either describing only the permit stage or not describing reality. If you need someone starting for a seasonal peak, count backwards six months from the start date and that is your deadline to begin.</p>
